Our Computer Lab in Zimbabwe

Our Accomplishments

What We Built Together

From preparing the room to running the first lessons — here is what we achieved step by step.

Room Preparation

We furnished the lab with locally-made wooden desks and chairs, creating a proper classroom from scratch.

Done

Solar Energy

Solar panels were installed on the roof — the lab now runs entirely on clean, independent energy.

Done

Security

Burglar bars and CCTV cameras were fitted to protect all equipment around the clock.

Done

Starlink Internet

A Starlink satellite dish now gives the school fast, reliable internet — for the very first time.

Done

Computers Delivered

10 computers were shipped from Europe and monitors sourced locally, fully equipping the lab.

Done

Technical Setup

Windows, educational software and child-safe browser settings were configured on every machine.

Done

ICT Teacher Hired

After interviews, a qualified ICT teacher was selected to run structured lessons full-time.

Done

First ICT Lessons

Children held a mouse for the very first time and started learning to type, draw, and explore.

Done

Looking Ahead

Next Steps — 2026 & Beyond

The lab is running — now we need to sustain and grow it. Here's what comes next.

1

Secure Sponsorship for the ICT Teacher's Salary

Identify donors, partners, or CSR programs to fund the full-time ICT educator — ensuring long-term sustainability of the digital learning program.

2

Ship Remaining Equipment & Classroom Enhancements

Ship printer, projector, mouse pads, and USB sticks; purchase locally a shelf for storing equipment, dust-proof computer covers, and ICT schoolbooks.

3

Launch Full ICT Curriculum

Begin the first official teaching term of ICT as a structured subject at Bongai Shamwari — turning the pilot lessons into a complete digital education program.

Help Us Keep the Lab Running

Your donation directly funds the ICT teacher's salary, new equipment, and the continued education of children who have never had access to a computer before.
